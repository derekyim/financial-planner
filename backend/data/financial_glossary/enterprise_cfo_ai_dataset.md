# SECTION 1 --- CORE FINANCIAL CONCEPTS

## Revenue Model (Ecommerce Standard)

Revenue = Orders × Average Order Value

Orders = Traffic × Conversion Rate

Average Order Value = (Product Revenue − Discounts + Shipping Income) /
Orders

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Contribution Margin

Contribution Margin = Revenue − COGS − Fulfillment − Payment Fees

If CAC \> Contribution Margin → growth destroys cash.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## EBITDA

EBITDA = Revenue − Operating Costs + Depreciation + Amortization

Used for:

-   valuation multiples
-   lender covenants
-   operational comparison

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Cash Conversion Cycle

CCC = DIO + DSO − DPO

DIO = Avg Inventory / COGS × 365\
DSO = AR / Revenue × 365\
DPO = AP / COGS × 365

Lower CCC improves liquidity.

# SECTION 3 --- CFO TROUBLESHOOTING PLAYBOOK

## EBITDA Falling While Revenue Rising

Possible causes:

-   CAC rising faster than sales
-   higher discounting
-   freight cost inflation
-   fulfillment inefficiency
-   inventory write-offs

Diagnostic steps:

1 check CAC trend\
2 check discount %\
3 check freight per order\
4 review inventory adjustments

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Cash Declining Despite Profit

Possible causes:

-   inventory growth
-   delayed receivables
-   supplier prepayments
-   capex spending

Diagnostic steps:

1 compare inventory vs prior months\
2 review AR aging\
3 inspect PO timing\
4 review capex ledger

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## High Revenue but Low Cash

Typical ecommerce cause:

inventory buildup before seasonal demand.

Rule:

Profit ≠ Cash.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# SECTION 4 --- INVENTORY & DEMAND FORECASTING PLAYBOOK

## Reorder Point

Reorder Point = Average Daily Sales × Lead Time + Safety Stock

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Purchase Requirement

Required Purchase = Forecast Sales + Target Ending Inventory − Beginning
Inventory

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Inventory Risk Signals

-   inventory growing faster than sales
-   stockouts on top SKUs
-   aging stock \> 180 days
-   frequent freight expediting
