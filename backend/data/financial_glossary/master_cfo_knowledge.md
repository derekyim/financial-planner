## Financial Planning & Analysis (FP&A)

### Core Responsibilities

-   Forecast revenue, expenses, and cash
-   Monitor performance vs plan
-   Identify operational drivers
-   Support pricing, hiring, and inventory decisions
-   Maintain liquidity runway visibility

### Standard CFO Reporting Pack

**Weekly** - Cash balance and runway - Sales vs forecast - Marketing
efficiency dashboard - Inventory risk summary

**Monthly** - Income Statement vs Budget - Cash Flow Forecast (13 week +
annual) - KPI bridge (Revenue → EBITDA → Cash) - Variance explanations

## Core Ecommerce Revenue Model

Revenue = Orders × Average Order Value

Where:

Orders = Traffic × Conversion Rate

Average Order Value = (Product Revenue − Discounts + Shipping Income) /
Orders

## Cash Conversion Cycle

Cash Conversion Cycle = DIO + DSO − DPO

DIO = Avg Inventory / COGS × 365\
DSO = Accounts Receivable / Revenue × 365\
DPO = Accounts Payable / COGS × 365

Lower CCC improves liquidity.

Negative CCC possible for strong ecommerce brands.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## EBIT vs EBITDA

EBIT = Revenue − Operating Expenses

EBITDA = EBIT + Depreciation + Amortization

Used for: - valuation multiples - loan covenants - operational
profitability comparison

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Contribution Margin

Contribution Margin = Revenue − COGS − Fulfillment − Payment Fees

This metric determines marketing scalability.

If CAC \> Contribution Margin → growth destroys cash.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Cash Forecast Framework

Ending Cash = Beginning Cash + Cash Inflows − Cash Outflows

Inflows: - customer receipts - financing - tax refunds

Outflows: - inventory purchases - payroll - marketing - rent - debt
service

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Inventory Purchase Planning

Required Purchase = Forecast Sales + Target Ending Inventory − Beginning
Inventory

Safety stock should reflect: - supplier reliability - sales volatility -
seasonality risk

## ERP Operational Best Practices

### QuickBooks

-   Separate revenue accounts by channel
-   Reconcile payment processors weekly
-   Sync ecommerce platform daily
-   Lock prior months after closing

### NetSuite

-   Use Location for warehouse
-   Use Class for product category
-   Use Department for business unit
-   Maintain saved searches for KPI reporting

### Xero

-   Use tracking categories for channels
-   Maintain separate clearing accounts
-   Reconcile bank feeds daily

## Spreadsheet Modeling Rules

-   Never hardcode numbers in formulas
-   Separate Inputs / Calculations / Outputs
-   Use consistent time columns across sheets
-   Use named ranges for major drivers
-   Maintain audit checks for totals

## Warehouse Performance KPIs

-   Orders picked per labor hour
-   Picking accuracy rate
-   Fulfillment cycle time
-   Cost per order shipped

Operational improvements: - Slot fast SKUs near packing - Batch pick
when possible - Separate wholesale vs DTC workflows - Barcode scan all
outbound shipments

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Price Elasticity

Elasticity = % Change in Quantity / % Change in Price

Elastic (\>1): demand sensitive\
Inelastic (\<1): demand stable

Test price changes gradually and monitor:

-   conversion rate
-   revenue
-   gross profit
-   refund behavior

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## CFO Diagnostic Playbook

### EBITDA Falling While Revenue Rising

Check: - CAC trend - discount % - freight cost per order - inventory
write-offs

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Cash Declining Despite Profit

Check: - inventory growth - AR aging delays - large supplier
prepayments - capital expenditures

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### High Revenue but Low Cash

Typical cause: inventory buildup before seasonal demand.

Always model inventory purchasing separately from P&L.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Strategic Finance Rules for Ecommerce

-   Profit does not equal cash
-   Inventory mistakes destroy liquidity faster than marketing mistakes
-   CAC must always be evaluated against contribution margin
-   Forecast inventory purchases before approving marketing scale
-   Always model downside scenarios before growth scenarios
